The Problem

Our names are Kaushik, Alan, and Tiberius and we are group of student currently attending Dublin High School. Each of us have had our own unique problems regarding the disfunctionality of our alarm clock. Once the alarm goes off, turning it off is very convinient and this does not actually wake a person up. In fact, it has become so convenient that it has become an instinct to turn off the alarm for many people, which goes against the purpose of one. Thus, people could easily just go back to sleep after snoozing their alarm, making them late to either their job, school, or any activity they have planned. In conclusion, we believe that the conventional alarm clock is ineffective in waking up people, as it does not require much effort to turn it off.

Our Solution

To solve this problem, we decided to create an alarm clock that stimulates the user's brain while getting them physically active. In order to do this, our alarm clock has 2 components: the clock and a sensor. Once the alarm goes off, the user will have to walk to the motion sensor in order to disarm it. Once the motion sensor has detected motion, it will then direct the user back to the clock, which will then display a simple math problem. Once the user solves it, it will then turn off the alarm. This practice ensures that the person is both fully physically and mentally awake when they start their day. They will have no desire to go back to sleep, which fulfill's the purpose of an alarm clock.
